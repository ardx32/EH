Practical Implementation: ARP Poisoning on Windows

The objective is to position your Windows machine logically between a target victim (e.g., another PC on the same Wi-Fi) and the network gateway (the router), allowing you to intercept and redirect their traffic.

Step 1: Network Discovery (Mapping the Subnet)
1.	Open Cain and Abel and ensure the Sniffer is activated (the network card icon on the top toolbar).
2.	Navigate to the Sniffer tab and click on the Hosts sub-tab at the bottom.
3.	Click the "+" (Add to list) icon. Select "MAC Scanner" and scan your subnet (e.g., 192.168.1.1 to 192.168.1.254).
4.	The tool will populate a list of all active IP addresses and their corresponding MAC (hardware) addresses on your local network.

Step 2: Configuring the Poisoning Matrix
1.	Switch to the APR (ARP Poison Routing) sub-tab at the bottom of the screen.
2.	Click anywhere in the top, empty pane, then click the "+" (Add to list) icon.
3.	A routing matrix will appear with two columns.
o	Left Column: Select the IP address of your Gateway (Router).
o	Right Column: Select the IP address of your Target Victim.
4.	Click OK. This configures Cain to tell the router that your machine is the victim, and to tell the victim that your machine is the router.

Step 3: Execution and Interception
1.	Click the Start/Stop ARP icon (the yellow/black radioactive symbol on the top toolbar).
2.	Result: Your Windows system is now broadcasting forged ARP replies. Traffic from the victim is now flowing through your machine before reaching the internet.
3.	You can analyze intercepted passwords or VoIP traffic in the Passwords or VoIP tabs at the bottom.
